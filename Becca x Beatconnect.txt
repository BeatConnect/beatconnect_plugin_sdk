BECCA X BEAT CONNECT




Plugin name: Becca Tone Amp


Concept


Becca Tone Amp is my go-to guitar tone in a box. I wanted to make something that really captures the warmth, the feel, and the emotion I try to get every time I play. The idea is that someone plugs in and instantly feels inspired, like they’re jamming with me. The plugin is clean but rich, and gives players room to experiment without feeling overwhelmed.
This is my musical philosophy: tone isn’t just about how it sounds but it’s about how it makes you feel. The plugin is buttery, warm, slightly driven, and really expressive. It works for  soulful fusion, funk, R&B, indie pop, psychedelic rock, blues and melodic playing, basically anything I normally play. Should make players feel like they’re playing with passion and intention, not just mechanically.


Core Tone DNA


First impression → As soon as you start playing, you should feel like you can jam forever. The tone hits in the right spot: warm, smooth, slightly driven, not too clean, not too dirty, just perfect for almost any melodic song.


Clean tone → Thick, warm and rounded rather than glassy and flat.


Influences → Mateus Asato for warmth, Cory Wong for funk precision, John Mayer for smooth expressiveness and slightly overdriven stuff. Hendrix-style lead and rhythm fuzz shows up lightly in some presets.


Guitar compatibility → Works amazing with single coils in the neck/middle and humbuckers on the bridge, but especially with coil-split guitars, which I love. Middle + bridge combos in single coils are my go-to for funk and rhythmic playing, while neck and middle with humbuckers are my go-to for everything else I play.


Amp feel and dynamics


Drive / Warmth → Slight edge-of-breakup warmth is built-in. The gain knob lets you push it into overdriven, harmonic-rich and slightly fuzzy territory.


Compression → The built-in is super subtle, however you should be able to increase it using a knob on the amp (ideal for funk/jam style). Some presets will have more or less compression built in.


Effects


* Chorus, tremolo, delay, reverb, and light distortion live in a separate pedal section. Compression is built into the amp at a subtle default level but users can tweak it with a knob on the amp itself. Advanced users could also have the option to modify compression in pedal form (as a separate effect) if desired.
* Some presets will have some FX pedals already turned on by default. (e.g. 80s preset will have chorus already on)
* Amp knobs do not control effects, so the plugin stays simple and beginner friendly.
* Effects can be stacked, tweaked, and experimented with, that way users have freedom to play around.


Interface general idea


* Minimal and easy to use  (I don’t want anyone to feel intimidated)


* Advanced players can tweak gain, compression, pedal FX, eq, room, cab, for full control.


* Only a few main knobs on the amp itself: lows, mids, high, level, volume, presence, gain, compression


* Pedals are in a separate section, visually clear, so you can tweak without cluttering the amp

User Experience Goals


* Beginners should be able to plug in, start playing, and feel instantly musical.


* Experts can tweak gain, compression, pedals, and tone, and optionally access an advanced EQ & room page for deeper customization.












Advanced Controls / Expert Options


For those who want to dive deeper, the plugin also has an optional Advanced Controls page. Users can still plug in and play immediately, but there will be an advanced page for those who want to dig deeper into shaping their tone.


The Advanced Controls page is initially greyed out to keep the interface approachable and not intimidating. Users can click a small toggle labeled “Expert Mode” in a different window (similar to how fx pedals are placed in a different section) to unlock all advanced features. This ensures beginners aren’t overwhelmed, while giving more experienced players full control over their sound.


The advanced page includes:


* EQ: Full 8-band EQ: ~60Hz → 12kHz, to control highs, mids, and lows. Adjust to taste to shape your tone for different songs or styles. Bands: 60Hz, 120Hz, 250Hz, 500Hz, 1kHz, 2kHz, 4kHz, 8kHz


* Room: Adjust mic placement (closer = more direct sound, slightly brighter and punchier, more detail in pick attack; further = Softer, airier tone with more room ambiance → feels like you’re in a bigger space) 
* Presence: control how forward or distant the amp feels in the mix. (boosting presence = guitar cut through the mix with clarity; reducing presence = softer and more blended sound). 
* CAB / IR (Built-in): Each preset comes with a subtle cabinet vibe that gives its tone personality and realism. Expert Mode lets you tweak:
1. Mic placement & angle: closer/farther or straight-on/off-axis for punch vs. smoothness.
2. Cab style (optional presets): small selection like “vintage,” “modern,” or “tight,” depending on the preset.


These controls add depth, space, and realism without changing the core tone. Each preset already has a cab base sound so the plugin sounds great straight out of the box.
